Figure 3.1 Contracts with risk-sharing elements

(Adapted from Simchi-Levi et al., 2008, Chapter 4)

Buy-back contracts: seller agrees to buy back any unsold goods at an agreed price if not sold, decreasing the likelihood of out-of-stocks (more than compensating increased supplier risk)

Revenue sharing contracts: buyer shares some of the revenue with the seller in return for a discount in the wholesale purchase price

Quantity flexibility contracts: supplier will buy back up to a certain amount at full refund (whereas a buy back contract is for supplier to take back all unsold items at a discounted value)

Sales rebate: a rebate for all items sold above a certain quantity

Make to order versus a make to stock contract: the supplier risk is reduced considerably if only making to order compared than to forecast.
